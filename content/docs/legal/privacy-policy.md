# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** March 1, 2026
**Last Updated:** March 1, 2026

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how MuriData ("Company," "we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, discloses, and protects information in connection with your use of the MuriData website, protocol, and related services (collectively, the "Services").

By accessing or using the Services, you consent to the collection and use of information as described in this Policy.

## 1. Information We Collect

### 1.1. Information You Provide

- **Wallet Address.** When you connect a cryptocurrency wallet to the Services, we receive your public wallet address. We do not collect or have access to your private keys.
- **Transaction Data.** When you interact with the MuriData smart contracts, transaction details are recorded on the public blockchain. This includes wallet addresses, transaction amounts, and smart contract interactions.
- **Communications.** If you contact us via email or other communication channels, we collect the content of your communications and any information you voluntarily provide.

### 1.2. Information Collected Automatically

- **Usage Data.** We collect information about how you interact with the Services, including pages visited, features used, and time spent on the Services.
- **Device Information.** We collect information about the device you use to access the Services, including device type, operating system, browser type, and screen resolution.
- **IP Address.** We collect your IP address for security, fraud prevention, and analytics purposes.
- **Cookies and Similar Technologies.** We use cookies and similar tracking technologies as described in our [Cookie Policy](/docs/legal/cookie-policy).

### 1.3. Blockchain Data

The MuriData protocol operates on a public blockchain. All on-chain transactions, including storage orders, proof submissions, staking operations, and token transfers, are publicly visible and immutable. This data is not collected by us but is inherent to the operation of the blockchain.

## 2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

2.1. **Service Provision.** To operate, maintain, and improve the Services.

2.2. **Security.** To detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, security breaches, and technical issues.

2.3. **Analytics.** To understand how users interact with the Services and to improve user experience.

2.4. **Communications.** To respond to your inquiries, provide customer support, and send service-related notices.

2.5. **Legal Compliance.** To comply with applicable laws, regulations, legal processes, or governmental requests.

## 3. Legal Basis for Processing (EEA/UK Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA) or the United Kingdom, our legal basis for processing your personal data includes:

3.1. **Contractual Necessity.** Processing necessary to perform our obligations under the Terms of Service.

3.2. **Legitimate Interests.** Processing necessary for our legitimate interests, including fraud prevention, security, and service improvement, where such interests are not overridden by your data protection rights.

3.3. **Consent.** Where you have provided consent to the processing, such as for non-essential cookies.

3.4. **Legal Obligation.** Processing necessary to comply with legal obligations to which we are subject.

## 4. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your information in the following circumstances:

4.1. **Service Providers.** We may share information with third-party service providers who assist us in operating the Services, including hosting providers, analytics services, and security vendors. These providers are contractually obligated to use your information solely for the purposes of providing services to us.

4.2. **Legal Requirements.** We may disclose information if required to do so by law or in response to valid legal process, including subpoenas, court orders, or government requests.

4.3. **Protection of Rights.** We may disclose information to protect the rights, property, or safety of the Company, our users, or the public.

4.4. **Business Transfers.** In the event of a merger, acquisition, reorganization, or sale of assets, your information may be transferred as part of the transaction. We will notify you of any such transfer and any choices you may have regarding your information.

4.5. **Public Blockchain.** Information recorded on the blockchain is publicly accessible. This includes wallet addresses and transaction data associated with your use of the protocol.

## 5. Data Retention

5.1. We retain your personal information for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including to satisfy legal, accounting, or reporting obligations.

5.2. Usage data and analytics information are retained for a maximum of twenty-four (24) months.

5.3. Information recorded on the blockchain is permanent and immutable by nature and cannot be deleted.

5.4. When personal information is no longer required, we will securely delete or anonymize it.

## 6. Data Security

6.1. We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction.

6.2. Security measures include, but are not limited to: encryption of data in transit (TLS/SSL), access controls, regular security assessments, and secure development practices.

6.3. Despite our efforts, no method of electronic transmission or storage is completely secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security of your information.

## 7. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have the following rights regarding your personal information:

7.1. **Access.** The right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.

7.2. **Rectification.** The right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal information.

7.3. **Erasure.** The right to request deletion of your personal information, subject to certain exceptions (including blockchain data, which cannot be erased).

7.4. **Restriction.** The right to request restriction of processing of your personal information.

7.5. **Portability.** The right to receive your personal information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

7.6. **Objection.** The right to object to the processing of your personal information based on legitimate interests.

7.7. **Withdrawal of Consent.** Where processing is based on consent, the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@muri.moe. We will respond to your request within thirty (30) days.

## 8. International Data Transfers

8.1. Your information may be transferred to and processed in countries other than your country of residence. These countries may have data protection laws that differ from your jurisdiction.

8.2. Where we transfer personal data outside the EEA or UK, we implement appropriate safeguards, including Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission.

## 9. Children's Privacy

The Services are not directed to individuals under the age of 18. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that a child has provided us with personal information, we will take steps to delete such information.

The Services may contain links to third-party websites or services. This Policy does not apply to third-party websites or services, and we are not responsible for the privacy practices of such third parties. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party services you access.

We may update this Policy from time to time. Material changes will be communicated through the Services or by other reasonable means. The "Last Updated" date at the top of this Policy indicates when it was last revised. Your continued use of the Services after any changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated Policy.
